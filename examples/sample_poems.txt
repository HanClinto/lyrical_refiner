EXAMPLE 1: Perfect AABB Rhyme
The cat sat on the mat
The rat wore a fancy hat
The dog ran through the fog
The hog jumped like a frog

EXAMPLE 2: Strong Alliteration
Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers
A peck of pickled peppers Peter Piper picked

EXAMPLE 3: Internal Rhyme
I'm drastically plastic and enthusiastic
My tactics are fantastic and bombastic

EXAMPLE 4: Shakespeare's Sonnet 18 (first quatrain)
Shall I compare thee to a summer's day
Thou art more lovely and more temperate
Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May
And summer's lease hath all too short a date

EXAMPLE 5: Mosaic Rhyme (Eminem - Lose Yourself)
His palms are sweaty, knees weak, arms are heavy
There's vomit on his sweater already, mom's spaghetti
He's nervous, but on the surface he looks calm and ready
To drop bombs, but he keeps on forgetting
